# Hirec TDS drafting adjustments recorded from user-modified Hirec-R_TDS_v2.0

## Source and status

This note records drafting preferences extracted from the user-modified `Hirec-R_TDS_v2.0` file and applies them as future drafting guidance for Hirec-family technical data sheets and related coating documents.

Status: approved as drafting guidance by explicit user instruction, 10.07.2026.
Scope: drafting style, document structure and claim-control preferences only. This note does not approve product performance claims, warranty terms, SDS/CLP/REACH conclusions, regulatory conclusions, commercial commitments or customer-facing guarantees.

## Structural preferences

1. Prefer a conventional numbered TDS structure rather than an SDS-like section framing.
2. Start with a plain title line, then numbered major sections and subsections.
3. Use clear section grouping:
   - Product Identification & Document Control
   - Product Description & Key Benefits
   - Composition & General Characteristics
   - Typical Physical & Chemical Properties
   - Performance Data
   - Application Instructions
   - Packaging, Storage & Shelf Life
   - Handling, Safety & Environmental Information (summary)
   - Environmental & Sustainability Information
   - Quality Assurance & Testing
   - Legal Notices, Warranty & Document Use
4. Keep content customer-facing and technical-commercial, while referencing the SDS for regulatory safety details.

## Content preferences

1. Use direct product statements and compact sentences.
2. Separate technical contact and sales contact.
3. Include manufacturer and supplier identification explicitly.
4. Include document number, version, issue date, revision date and language/region scope.
5. State recommended uses and restrictions early.
6. Include practical application detail sections:
   - substrate suitability
   - surface preparation
   - mixing
   - thinning/dilution
   - application methods
   - recommended film thickness / application rate
   - recoat interval
   - cleaning of tools
   - application conditions
7. Include explicit performance caveats:
   - typical values only
   - project-specific validation required
   - warranty/performance commitment only by written project-specific agreement
8. Include explicit safety summary, but avoid turning the TDS into a full SDS.
9. Include QA/testing section with typical test methods and inspection items.
10. Include shelf-life/storage wording, even if shelf life must be confirmed from supplier label/SDS.
11. Include environmental/VOC note in short summary form.

## Style preferences

1. Dense but readable technical text.
2. Prefer numbered sections over narrative blocks.
3. Use short bullet-style lines for features/benefits and test references.
4. Prefer clear caveat language over speculative claims.
5. Use canonical product naming consistently.

## Claim and approval controls

1. Do not convert drafting guidance into approval for customer-facing performance claims.
2. Do not state guaranteed service life, fixed warranty, fixed reapplication interval or universal suitability unless a current approved source and project-specific commercial approval exist.
3. Keep SDS, CLP, REACH, dangerous-goods, transport, regulatory and legal conclusions tied to the current SDS/regulatory source and qualified review where required.
4. For Hirec family documents, use canonical Ea product names: Hirec-R, Hirec-RAS, Hirec PFW9 and Hirec PFS10.
5. Legacy names may be retained only as source references or revision notes where necessary for traceability.

## Practical TDS drafting pattern to follow

When creating or revising Hirec-family TDS documents, use the following order unless the user provides another template:

1. Header/title and document control.
2. Product identification, use area and restrictions.
3. Manufacturer/supplier/contact details.
4. Short product description and key benefits.
5. Composition/general characteristics and physical/chemical data.
6. Drying/curing and storage/service notes.
7. Performance data with caveats.
8. Application instructions in practical subsections.
9. Packaging/storage/shelf life.
10. Handling/safety/environmental summary.
11. QA/testing references.
12. Legal notices/warranty boundary statement.

## Future-use instruction

For future Hirec-family technical documents, use this note as drafting-style guidance in addition to current product knowledge, current SDS/TDS/application evidence, source authority rules and explicit user instructions.
