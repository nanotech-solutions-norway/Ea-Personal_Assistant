## Approved application examples

### UAV

Use only one UAV-focused card. Literature examples may show approximately 28% rotor-thrust loss during the first 60 seconds and approximately 184% higher power demand in the cited test context. These values must be labelled as literature evidence and not as Hirec product-performance data.

### Wind turbine

Use only one wind-turbine evidence card. The field study may be shown as:
- Before coating: 61.6 MWh icing-related loss, 9.7% of 516.6 MWh total generation.
- After coating: 39.0 MWh icing-related loss, 4.7% of 736.8 MWh total generation.
- Icing exposure: 357.5 hours before and 328.8 hours after.

The card must state that the observation periods and operating conditions differed and that the result is not a controlled one-to-one comparison.

### Radome / microwave link

Use only one radome-focused card. Ericsson Microwave Outlook 2023 may be used to show:
- Up to 6 dB less fading in the coated example on a 200 m E-band hop.
- Approximately 4 dB attenuation with dry snow and more than 9 dB with wet snow for the uncoated example.
- A Nordic operational example where site visits fell to one-third of the previous year.

These are external evidence examples and must not be presented as universal Hirec results.

## Hirec-family boundaries

### Hirec-R
- Solvent-based professional high-repellency route.
- Current HIREC catalogue identifies a self-cleaning function.
- Dedicated primer/application route may be required.

### Hirec-RAS
- Aerosol version of Hirec-R.
- Intended for small-area application, repair and maintenance contexts.
- Current SDS controls aerosol handling and transport.

### Hirec PFW9
- Water-based, PFAS-free high-repellency route.
- Relevant where a water-based application system is preferred.
- Current TDS values used in the presentation: matte-white liquid, pH 7.5, relative density 1.02.

### Hirec PFS10
- Solvent-based, PFAS-free high-repellency route.
- Relevant where a solvent-based film-formation route is technically appropriate.
- Current TDS values used in the presentation: white liquid, relative density 0.71.
