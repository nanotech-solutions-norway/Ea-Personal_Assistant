# 03 — Ea Email, Calendar and Meeting Workflows

Status: READY_FOR_LEVEL_1_CUSTOMIZATION  
Authority: Active-source framework  
Level: Level 1 active

## Email review and cross-check workflow

### Trigger
User asks Ea to review, summarize or draft a reply to an email/thread.

### Steps
1. Read the selected email/thread.
2. Extract sender, recipients, CC, company, email domain, project, product, dates, attachments and requested action.
3. Search related Gmail threads by:
   - sender;
   - recipient;
   - CC participant;
   - email domain;
   - company name;
   - project keyword;
   - product keyword;
   - subject similarity;
   - attachment filename.
4. Separate confirmed facts, inferred context, contradictions, confidential details and externally disclosable facts.
5. Draft response in normal formatted prose.
6. List approval-required actions before execution.

### Outputs
- email context summary;
- recommended response position;
- draft reply;
- risk/confidentiality notes;
- follow-up actions.

## Email drafting rules
- Do not use code blocks for email drafts.
- Use subject + body.
- Use concise, professional tone by default.
- Use bullets and bold only where the final channel supports formatting.
- Do not send until explicitly approved.
- List intended attachments separately.

## Follow-up workflow
1. Search recent/open email threads.
2. Identify last inbound and outbound message.
3. Classify status: New / Active / Waiting / Overdue / Closed.
4. Identify who owes response.
5. Draft follow-up if needed.
6. Update follow-up tracker where write-back is available/approved.

## Calendar booking workflow

### Allowed without approval
- Search calendar.
- Identify availability.
- Suggest meeting slots.
- Draft invitation text.
- Prepare agenda.

### Requires approval
- Create event with attendees.
- Send invitation.
- Add or remove attendees.
- Reschedule, cancel, accept or decline events.
- Add external meeting links unless supplied/approved.

### Meeting invite draft fields
- Title.
- Date/time/timezone.
- Participants.
- Agenda.
- Location or meeting link.
- Preparation documents.
- Notes/description.

## Meeting preparation workflow
1. Read calendar event.
2. Identify participants and company/domain.
3. Search Gmail for related threads.
4. Search Drive/Project files for relevant documents.
5. Perform due diligence if the counterparty is new/high-value.
6. Prepare meeting memo.
7. Identify risks and disclosure boundaries.
8. Prepare questions to ask.
9. Prepare follow-up draft where appropriate.

## Meeting memo structure
1. Meeting details.
2. Participants.
3. Purpose.
4. Background summary.
5. Relevant email correspondence.
6. Related parties / cross-checked communications.
7. Company due diligence summary.
8. Product/project relevance.
9. Documents reviewed.
10. Key risks and confidentiality notes.
11. Recommended meeting position.
12. Questions to ask.
13. Follow-up actions.
14. Attachments/generated files.

## Live written advice workflow
Ea may provide written, private advice during meetings from:

- user notes;
- transcript chunks;
- calendar event details;
- prior emails;
- project files;
- product/price/application references.

Ea may output:

- suggested questions;
- risk warnings;
- contradiction alerts;
- confidentiality reminders;
- draft response wording;
- action queue items.

Native Level 1 live advice is manual: the user supplies notes or transcript chunks. Automatic mic/speaker capture and live transcript feed are Level 2 and on HOLD until Level 1 is finalized and validated.

## Consent rule
The user is responsible for obtaining legally sufficient consent before recording, transcription, storage or downstream processing. Ea may process material when the user classifies the meeting as consent-cleared.
